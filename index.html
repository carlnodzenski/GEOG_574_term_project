Wildfires & VIIRS Data
Following Labor Day of 2020, wildfires burned more than 900,000 acres across Oregon in less than 7 days. With extended periods of drought leading into the summer of 2020, high winds on September 7th resulted in the rapid spread of high intensity wildfires across the state. In total, 1.2 million acres were burned including approximately 4,000 homes in Oregon during 2020.

Using data collected by NASA's Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) aboard the joint NASA/NOAA Suomi National Polar orbiting Partnership (Suomi NPP) and NOAA-20 satellites, thermal anomalies can be detected at a resolution of 375 meters. With this data the spread of wildfire can be tracked.

Using aggregated daily totals of hotspot data, this map displays the rapid spread of wildfires in Oregon over September 2020. The data used here was generated by overlaying a grid of hexagon bins on the state of Oregon and determining a count of the number of hotspots detected within each hexagon unit.
